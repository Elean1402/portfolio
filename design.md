# Design Brief

## Concept

An abandoned J-rock rehearsal shrine hidden after midnight.

The left side is bold and readable. The right side is chaotic and layered:
amps, drums, guitars, cables, flyers, feedback, and crooked signs.

The site should feel personal, loud, handmade, game-like, and slightly broken.

## Influences

- Underground Japanese live houses
- J-rock gig posters, album inserts, and rehearsal tapes
- Interactive 3D portfolio scenes
- Game save points and selectable world objects
- Abandoned music equipment and improvised stages

## Visual Style

- Dark midnight background
- Berry pink, mint, yellow, violet, and cream accents
- Irregular curves, rotations, overlaps, and torn shapes
- No clean grids or corporate dashboard styling
- No full-screen grain or scanline effects
- Solid, readable headline fonts

## Typography

- Headlines: `Archivo Black`
- Labels and small text: `IBM Plex Mono`
- Body copy: `Space Grotesk`

## Content and Voice

The writing should be informal, direct, personal, and slightly rough.

Developer experience should appear through the projects and writing—not through
a highly technical interface.

## Interactions

- Scene tilts slightly with pointer movement
- Number keys `1–4` open sections
- `Escape` returns home
- Arrow keys change projects
- Main sign toggles stage power
- Header button plays or stops the original music loop
- Audio must never autoplay

## Responsive Direction

On desktop, content stays on the left and the visual scene stays on the right.

On mobile, text remains the priority while the scene is cropped toward the
lower-right. Navigation and body copy must remain usable.
